Identifiability of Linear non-Gaussian Additive Model

Posted on 2017-04-12 |

This post shows the proof of the identifiability of Linear non-Gaussian Additive Model. [1]

Model definition

Let $X$ be the cause and $Y$ be effect ($X \to Y$) without loss of generality. The causal realtion between them is given by
\begin{align}
Y = a X + \epsilon, \tag{1}
\label{df}
\end{align}

where $a$ is a constant coefficient and $\epsilon$ is a non-Gaussian noise which is independent of $X$. The model in \eqref{df} is called Linear non-Gaussian Additive Model (LiNGAM).[1]

Identifiability

Showing the identifiablity of a causal model is actually exploiting its cause-effect asymmetry, which means the property that holds only in the true causal direction and is violated in the anti-causal direction, from the given model. Causal models with cause-effect asymmetry are said to be identifiable.

The identifiability of LiNGAM is proved using Darmois - Skitovitch theorem, which reads,

Darmois-Skitovitch theorem (D-S Theorem) [2] [3] Define two random variables $L_{1}$ and $L_{2}$ as linear combinations of independent random variables $s_{i}, (i=1, …, q)$:
\begin{align}
L_{1} = \sum_{i=1}^{q} \alpha_{i} s_{i}, \quad L_{2} = \sum_{i=1}^{q} \beta_{i} s_{i},
\end{align}

where $\alpha_{i}$ and $\beta_{i}$ are constant coefficients. Then, if $L_{1}$ and $L_{2}$ are independent, all variables $s_{j}$ for which $\alpha_{j} \beta_{j} \neq 0$ are Gaussian.

In other words, this theorem means that if there exists a non-Gaussian $s_{j}$ for which $\alpha_{j} \beta_{j} \neq 0$, $L_{1}$ and $L_{2}$ are dependent.

Now we assume there exist a backward model $X = bY + \tilde{\epsilon}$ for a forward LiNGAM, then in the forward model $Y = aX + \epsilon$ we have
\begin{align}
s_{1} & := X \\
s_{2} & := Y - a X
\end{align}

where $s_{1}$ and $s_{2}$ should be independent according to the assumption of LiNGAM. Similarly in the backward model we have
\begin{align}
L_{1} & := Y \\
L_{2} & := X - b Y
\end{align}

It is obvious that both $L_{1}$ and $L_{2}$ can be written as a linear combination of $s_{1}$ and $s_{2}$, i.e.
\begin{align}
L_{1} & = a s_{1} + s_{2} \\
L_{2} & = (1 - ba)s_{1} -b s_{2}
\end{align}

Since $s_{2}$ is non-Gaussian, $L_{1}$ and $L_{2}$ will be dependent according to D-S theorem as long as $b \neq 0$, which is ensured by the existence of the causal relation between $X$ and $Y$. Thus, we conclude that the cause and the noise in the backward model will be dependent if the forward model follows a LiNGAM. This asymmetry of LiNGAM allows us to infer its causal direction.

Reference

  1. Kano, Y., & Shimizu, S. (2003). Causal inference using nonnormality. In Proceedings of the international symposium on science of modeling, the 30th anniversary of the information criterion (pp. 261-270).
  2. Darmois, G. (1953). Analyse générale des liaisons stochastiques: etude particulière de l’analyse factorielle linéaire. Revue de l’Institut international de statistique, 2-8.
  3. Skitovitch, V. P. (1953). On a property of the normal distribution. DAN SSSR, 89, 217-219.
