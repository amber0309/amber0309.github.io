Derivation of Theorem 1 in ANM

Posted on 2015-12-26 |

This post shows the derivation step by step of the condition that ANM [1] is not identifiable (Eq. (4) in [1]).

Identifiability of ANM

The identifiability results of ANM is given by the following theorem in [1]

Theorem 1 Let the joint probability density of $x$ and $y$ be given by
\begin{align}
p(x,y) = p_{\epsilon}(y-f(x))p_{x}(x), \tag{2}
\end{align}

where $p_{\epsilon}$, $p_{x}$ are probability densities on $\mathbb{R}$. If there is a backward model of the same form, i.e.,
\begin{align}
p(x,y) = p_{\varepsilon}(x-g(y))p_{y}(y), \tag{3}
\end{align}

then, denoting $\nu := \log p_{\epsilon}$ and $\xi := \log p_{x}$, the triple $(f, p_{x}, p_{\epsilon})$ must satisfy the following differential equation for all $x$, $y$ with $\nu^{\prime\prime}(y-f(x)) f^{\prime}(x) \neq 0$:
\begin{align}
\xi^{\prime\prime\prime} = \xi^{\prime\prime}\left( -\frac{\nu^{\prime\prime\prime} f^{\prime}}{\nu^{\prime\prime} } + \frac{f^{\prime\prime}}{f^{\prime}} \right) - 2\nu^{\prime\prime} f^{\prime\prime} f^{\prime} + \nu^{\prime} f^{\prime\prime\prime} + \frac{ \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime\prime\prime} f^{\prime} f^{\prime\prime} }{ \nu^{\prime\prime} } - \frac{ \nu^{\prime} \left( f^{\prime\prime}\right)^{2} }{f^{\prime}}, \tag{4}
\label{condition}
\end{align}

where the arguments $y-f(x)$, $x$, and $x$ for $\nu$, $\xi$, $f$ and their derivatives are skipped for clarity. Moreover, if for a fixed pair $(f, \nu)$ there exists $y\in \mathbb{R}$ such that $\nu^{\prime\prime} (y-f(x)) f^{\prime}(x) \neq 0$ for all but a countable set of points $x \in \mathbb{R}$, the set of all $p_{x}$ for which $p$ has a backward model is contained in a 3-dimensional affine space.

Derivation of Eq. (4)

Now we show how Eq. (4) is derived. Let
\begin{align}
\pi(x, y) := \log p(x,y)=\nu (y-f(x)) + \xi(x), \tag{5}
\end{align}

and $\tilde{\nu}:= \log p_{\tilde{n}}$, $\eta := \log p_{y}$. If the backward model exists, which means Eq. (3) holds, then
\begin{align}
\pi(x, y) := \log p(x,y)=\tilde{\nu} (x-g(y)) + \eta(y). \tag{6}
\end{align}

In the causal direction ($x \to y$), taking the derivatives of Eq. (5) we have,
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x \partial y} & = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) \right] \\
& = -\nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x))f^{\prime} (x),
\end{align}

and
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x^{2}} & = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \xi^{\prime}(x) - \nu{\prime}(y-f(x))f^{\prime} (x) \right] \\
& = \xi^{\prime \prime}(x) - \left[ f^{\prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) - (f^{\prime}(x))^{2} \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) \right] \\
& = \xi^{\prime \prime}(x) - f^{\prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) + (f^{\prime}(x))^{2} \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)).
\end{align}

Then
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left( \frac{\partial^{2} \pi / \partial x^{2}}{ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y } \right) & = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \frac{ \xi^{\prime \prime}(x) - f^{\prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) + (f^{\prime} (x))^{2} \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) }{-\nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x))f^{\prime} (x)} \right] \tag{7} \\
& = \frac{\frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x^{2} \right] \left[\partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y\right] - \left[ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x^{2} \right] \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y \right] }{ \left[ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y \right]^{2}}. \tag{8}
\end{align}

The derivative of the numerator of Eq. (7) is
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left[ \frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x^{2}} \right] & = \xi^{\prime \prime \prime} (x) - \left[ f^{\prime \prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) - f^{\prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) f^{\prime} (x) \right] + \\
& \quad \, \left[ 2f^{\prime} (x) f^{\prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) - \left( f^{\prime} (x) \right)^{2} \nu{\prime \prime \prime}(y-f(x)) f^{\prime} (x) \right] \\
& = \xi^{\prime \prime \prime} (x) - f^{\prime \prime \prime} (x) \nu{\prime}(y-f(x)) + 3 f^{\prime \prime} (x) f^{\prime} (x) \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) \\
& \quad \; - \left( f^{\prime} (x) \right)^{3} \nu{\prime \prime \prime}(y-f(x)) \\
& = \xi^{\prime \prime \prime} - f^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu{\prime} + 3 f^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} \nu{\prime \prime} - \left( f^{\prime} \right)^{3} \nu{\prime \prime \prime},
\end{align}

and the derivative of the denominator of Eq. (7) is
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left[ \frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x \partial y} \right] & = -\left[ \nu{\prime \prime \prime}(y-f(x)) (-1) f^{\prime} (x) f^{\prime} (x) + \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) f^{\prime \prime} (x) \right] \\
& = \nu{\prime \prime \prime}(y-f(x)) \left( f^{\prime} (x) \right)^{2} - \nu{\prime \prime}(y-f(x)) f^{\prime \prime} (x) \\
& = \nu{\prime \prime \prime} \left( f^{\prime} \right)^{2} - \nu{\prime \prime} f^{\prime \prime}.
\end{align}

By substituting the above results into Eq. (8), we have
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left( \frac{\partial^{2} \pi / \partial x^{2}}{ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y } \right) & = \frac{ \left( \xi^{\prime \prime \prime} - f^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} + 3 f^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} - \left( f^{\prime} \right)^{3} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \right) \left( -\nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} \right)}{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime} \right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime} \right)^{2} } \\
& \quad \; - \frac{ \left( \xi^{\prime \prime} - f^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} + \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} \nu^{\prime \prime} \right) \left( \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \left( f^{\prime} \right)^{2} - \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime \prime} \right) }{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime} \right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime} \right)^{2}} \\
& = \frac{ -\xi^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} + f^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} - 3 \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} f^{\prime \prime} \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} + \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{4} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} }{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } \\
& \quad \; - \frac{ \xi^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} - \xi^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime \prime} - \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} f^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} + \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime}}{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } \\
& \quad \; - \frac{ \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{4} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} - \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} f^{\prime \prime} \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} }{\left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2}} \\
& = \frac{ -\xi^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} + f^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime} - 2 \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} f^{\prime \prime} \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} - \xi^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } \\
& \quad \; + \frac{ \xi^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime \prime} + \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} f^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} - \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime} }{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } \\
& = -\xi^{\prime \prime \prime} \frac{1}{\nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime}} + \frac{f^{\prime \prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} }{\nu^{\prime \prime} f^{\prime}} - 2 f^{\prime \prime} - \xi^{\prime \prime} \frac{ \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} }{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } + \xi^{\prime \prime} \frac{ f^{\prime \prime} }{ \nu^{\prime \prime} \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2}} \\
& \quad \; + \frac{ f^{\prime \prime} \nu^{\prime} \nu^{\prime \prime \prime} }{ \left(\nu^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} } - \frac{ \left(f^{\prime \prime}\right)^{2} \nu^{\prime} }{ \nu^{\prime \prime} \left(f^{\prime}\right)^{2} }. \tag{9}
\end{align}

In the backward model ($y \to x$), taking the same derivatives of Eq. (6) we have,
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x \partial y} & = \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \left[ \tilde{\nu}{\prime}(x-g(y)) \right] \\
& = -\tilde{\nu}{\prime \prime}(x-g(y))g^{\prime} (y),
\end{align}

and
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial^{2} \pi}{\partial x^{2}} & = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \tilde{\nu}{\prime}(x-g(y)) \right] \\
& = \tilde{\nu}{\prime \prime}(x-g(y)).
\end{align}

Then
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left( \frac{\partial^{2} \pi / \partial x^{2}}{ \partial^{2} \pi / \partial x \partial y } \right) = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left[ \frac{\tilde{\nu}{\prime \prime}(x-g(y))}{-\tilde{\nu}{\prime \prime}(x-g(y))g^{\prime} (y)} \right] = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left[ \frac{-1}{g^{\prime} (y)} \right] = 0. \tag{10}
\end{align}

At last, by combining Eq. (9) and (10) we obtain the condition shown in Eq. (4).

References
